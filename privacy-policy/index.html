The short version

Buoy helps you see who's at your dog park right now and meet up. We built it privacy-first, and that's not marketing:

  • We never store your location. Your GPS coordinates are used for a single yes/no check ("are you inside this park?" / "which parks are near you?") and then thrown away. There is no column in our database that could hold a coordinate, so not even we can reconstruct where you've been from a check-in.
  • We don't track you. No analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking SDKs. We don't touch the device advertising identifier and show no tracking prompt — there's nothing to track.
  • We collect very little. A dog name, a chosen silhouette avatar (no photos), and the parks you check into. No human name, no photo, no phone number, no address book.
  • You're in control. Turn on ghost mode to go invisible, block anyone, delete your whole account from inside the app at any time, and export your data.

The rest of this policy is the detail behind those claims.

1. What we collect

Account & sign-in

You sign in with Sign in with Apple or Google. From that sign-in we receive and store an email address through our authentication provider (Supabase). With Sign in with Apple, this may be Apple's private relay address that hides your real email. We use the email only to create and secure your account; it is not shown anywhere in the app and we don't use it for marketing. We also store the account identifier (a UUID) that links your data together.

Your dog profile

  • Dog name — free text you enter (1–24 characters). It's shown to other users at the same park. Please don't put a person's name here.
  • Avatar — one of a fixed set of in-app silhouettes. No photos are collected or uploaded.

Check-ins & presence

When you check in, we store that you are at a particular park, with a timestamp and an expiry — specifically (your account ID, park ID, status, created time, expiry time). We do not store the latitude/longitude — there is no coordinate column on this record. "Status" is either here (you're physically at the park, geofence-verified) or heading (you're on your way).

When a check-in succeeds we also keep a measurement record of the visit — (your account ID, park ID, time, status, and a count of how many other non-blocked users were present at that moment). It likewise has no coordinate column, is never visible to other users or shown in the app, and exists only so we can compute aggregate pilot health metrics (like "what share of visits had another dog present"). Section 4 covers how long it is kept.

Precise location (used, never stored)

Buoy uses your device's precise location, foreground only (while the app is open). Your coordinates leave your device in only two situations:

  1. When you tap "I'm here" — to verify you're physically inside the park.
  2. When the app opens — to find parks near you.

In both cases your coordinates are used only to compute a result (a geofence check, or a distance filter) and are then immediately discarded — never written to any database (there is no column anywhere that could hold a coordinate) and never attached to your account. Buoy's own code does not log your coordinates. We never request background location and never continuously track your position. (Tapping "On my way" to say you're heading to a park sends no location at all.)

Invites, blocks, and reports

  • Invites — when you invite someone (or are invited) at a park, we store the two account IDs, the park, and the time. Only the two of you can see it.
  • Blocks — if you block someone, we store that. The block is one-directional and secret: the blocked person is not notified and can't see that you blocked them.
  • Reports — if you report someone, we store your report, including the reason (a preset category or free text you write), so we can review abuse. Please don't include other people's personal information in the text. Reports are visible only to us; the reported person is not told.

Notifications

If you allow notifications, we store a push token for your device so we can deliver invite alerts. It's removed when you delete your account.

What we do not collect

No photos. No human name. No phone number, contacts, or address book. No analytics, advertising, or tracking identifiers. No background location. No stored coordinates. No payment information (Buoy is free in v0).

2. How we use your data

Only to run the service: authenticate you; show who's at a park; let you check in, invite, block, and report; deliver invite notifications; and respond to abuse reports and support requests. We do not sell your data, and we do not use it for advertising or cross-app tracking.

3. Who we share it with (service providers)

We don't sell your data. We rely on a small set of processors to operate:

Two specifics worth calling out:

  • Invite notifications carry a dog name + park name. A push alert shows the sender's dog name and the park name (for example, a title "Rex wants to meet" and body "at Sunset Woods Park."). That text passes through Expo's and Apple's/Google's push systems and can appear on your lock screen. The sender's account ID is deliberately not included.
  • Map tiles reveal approximate location to OpenStreetMap. As with any map, loading tiles tells the tile server roughly what area you're viewing, along with your IP address. We don't send your account identity to them.

These providers process data under their own privacy policies.

4. How long we keep it

  • Live check-ins auto-expire. A here check-in lasts 90 minutes; a heading one lasts 30 minutes. After that a cleanup job deletes the record (it runs every 5 minutes). There is no "I'm here until I leave" — the presence other users can see is always short-lived by design.
  • The park-level measurement record is kept for up to 180 days. The visit record described in section 1 (park, time, status, count of others present — never coordinates) is retained for up to 180 days and then deleted automatically by the same cleanup job. It appears in your data export and is erased immediately if you delete your account.
  • Profile, invites, blocks, reports, push tokens persist until you delete them or delete your account.
  • Account deletion is immediate and permanent. From Settings → Delete my account, we hard-delete your account and cascade-delete your profile, check-ins, invites, blocks, reports, and push tokens. There is no grace period and no recovery. (Note: this also erases reports you filed and reports about you.) Some records outside our app database — your authentication provider's security logs, your device's notification history, and your Apple/Google sign-in records — are governed by those providers' policies.

5. Your choices & rights

  • Ghost mode — go invisible instantly; while on, you create no presence and don't appear at any park.
  • Block — remove someone from your views immediately.
  • Delete your account — in-app, anytime (see section 4).
  • Export your data — in-app, anytime: a JSON file of your profile, your check-in history (including expired ones), your invites, the blocks you created, the reports you filed, and your push tokens. (This export does not currently include the sign-in email held by our auth provider — request that from us directly at the contact above.)
  • Notifications & location — control both anytime in your device settings; revoking location disables check-in and nearby-park discovery.

Depending on where you live (e.g., EEA/UK or California), you may have additional rights to access, correct, or delete your data, or to object to processing. The in-app delete + export cover most of these; for anything else, contact us.

6. How we protect it

  • Sign-in only via Apple/Google — Buoy never sees a password.
  • Your session is stored in your device's secure keystore (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore), not in plain storage.
  • All traffic is encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS).
  • Database access is locked down with row-level security so users can only read their own data and the limited public presence at a park.
  • The "are you inside the park?" check runs on our server against trusted park boundaries, so it can't be spoofed by a modified app.

7. Children

Buoy is not directed to children under 18 and we don't knowingly collect their data. If you believe a child has created an account, contact us and we'll remove it.

8. Changes

We may update this policy. We'll change the "Effective date" and, for material changes, notify you in-app. Continued use after an update means you accept it.
